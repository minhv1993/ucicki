    Families
    As a big club, meeting people and getting to know people can be fairly difficult and intimidating. To alleviate the stress, our club is broken up into smaller groups of about 20 people called "families." These families have two board members that are "parents" of the group and a general member called a "co-big". Together, the board members and co-big provide an outlet to the rest of the club for the general members so that they can get to know everyone else without having to meet everyone all at one time. The "children" or members of these families benefit from having a group of people ready to serve together, lead together, eat together, or just hang out together. After a while, a club of 200+ people doesn't seem as big; and although these families change by the year, the bonds that are formed definitely extend beyond that.
